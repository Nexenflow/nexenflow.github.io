# 🧭 WEBSITE INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE (IA) — CACTUS INFORMATIQUE
## Version 1.0 — Strategic Navigation Blueprint
### Status: Draft for Validation

> **Objective**
>
> This document defines the future information architecture of the Cactus Informatique corporate website.
>
> It is **not a sitemap**, **not wireframes**, and **not page content**.
>
> It defines the navigation philosophy, information hierarchy, and user entry points that will guide every future design and development decision.

---

# 1. Vision

The website should no longer present Cactus as an IT service provider.

Instead, it should position Cactus as a **business transformation partner** that helps organizations:

- Modernize their information systems
- Secure their infrastructure
- Build reliable business applications
- Protect critical business operations
- Recover from critical IT situations
- Prepare for the future of Data & AI

The navigation must therefore follow the customer's mental model instead of Cactus' internal organization.

---

# 2. Navigation Philosophy

The website should answer six questions as quickly as possible.

## Can you solve my problem?

↓

## Have you already solved it?

↓

## Do you understand my industry?

↓

## How do you work?

↓

## Why should I trust you?

↓

## How can we start?

Every menu item should contribute to answering one of these questions.

Everything else becomes secondary.

---

# 3. Core Navigation Principles

The navigation should be:

- Business-first
- Outcome-oriented
- Technology-agnostic
- Simple
- Scalable
- SEO friendly
- Maximum two navigation levels
- Maximum six primary navigation items

# 12. UX Principles

Maximum six main navigation entries.

Maximum two dropdown levels.

One primary CTA.

Consistent navigation.

Every important page reachable within three clicks.

No duplicated content.

No empty pages.

No technical jargon in navigation.

Business language first.
